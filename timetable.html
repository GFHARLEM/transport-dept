What to Do in Case of a Road Accident

Follow these steps carefully to ensure safety and proper handling of the situation.

Stay Calm and Ensure Safety

Take a deep breath. Check yourself and others for injuries. If possible, move to a safe location away from traffic.

Turn On Hazard Lights

Immediately activate your vehicle's hazard lights to alert other drivers. Use reflective triangles if available.

Move Vehicle Off Road

If the vehicle is drivable and it's safe to do so, move it to the shoulder or a safe area away from traffic.

Call Emergency Services

Dial 999 for police, 112 for fire/ambulance, or use the emergency contacts above. Provide clear location details.

Record Important Details

Note location, time, vehicle registration numbers, insurance details, and take photos if it's safe to do so.

Do NOT Leave the Scene

Remain at the accident scene until authorities arrive and give you permission to leave, unless medical evacuation is needed.

Cooperate with Authorities

Provide accurate information to police and emergency responders. Follow their instructions carefully.

Road Safety & Preparedness Tips

Prevention is key. Follow these safety measures to minimize risks during travel.

First Aid Kit

Always carry a well-stocked first aid kit. Ensure university vehicles are equipped with medical supplies.

Vehicle Inspection

Before long trips, check spare tire, tools, fuel, oil, brakes, and lights. Report any issues to transport department.

Fire Safety

Ensure vehicles have functional fire extinguishers. Know how to use them and check expiry dates regularly.

Communication Preparedness

Keep your phone charged. Carry a power bank. Save emergency contacts in your phone with "ICE" (In Case of Emergency) prefix.

Travel Companions

Inform someone about your travel plans, route, and expected arrival time. Avoid traveling alone when possible.

Documentation

Carry identification, insurance documents, and university transport authorization when using university vehicles.
